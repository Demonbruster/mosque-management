This epic focuses on digitizing the day-to-day administrative, logistical, and physical property management tasks that keep the mosque running smoothly. Here is the detailed breakdown of the requirements to hand over to your development team:
1. Fixed Asset Management

- The system must replace informal tracking with a centralized digital inventory for all mosque property and equipment.
- Asset Profiles & Source Tracking: Every asset (e.g., electronics, furniture, property) must have a unique ID, recording its purchase price, acquisition date, and physical condition. Crucially, the system must track the "fund source" to designate whether the item was purchased with mosque funds or donated by a devotee.
- Maintenance & Lifecycle: The module must track warranties and Annual Maintenance Contracts (AMCs), generating automated maintenance reminders for equipment requiring regular servicing. It should also document official disposal workflows if an asset is sold or scrapped.

2. Building & Tenancy Management

- If the mosque owns commercial real estate or properties that it leases out for revenue, the system needs a robust tenancy module.
- Agreements & Security Deposits: The system must register tenants, store their identification documents, and generate formal tenancy agreements. It must track security deposits within the balance sheet and manage refunds or deductions when a tenant leaves.
- Rent Collection & Adjustments: The module must track monthly rent payments, automatically generate "Rent Due" and "Rent Analysis" reports for overdue payments, and allow admins to apply temporary rent discounts if approved by management.

3. Utensil & Equipment Rentals

- Mosques frequently lend or rent out large cooking pots or event materials to the community.
- Inventory & Issuance: The system must maintain a master stock list of all utensils (with rental prices and quantities). When issuing items to a non-member, the system should require them to link a registered mosque member as a "guarantor".
- Returns & Automated Penalties: When items are returned, the system must automatically calculate and apply penalty fees if the admin marks any items as damaged or missing. It should generate a pre-formatted issue/return voucher detailing these charges.

4. Life Events & Administrative Documentation

- The CRM must serve as the official registry for the Mahalla's life events and administrative certificates.
- Marriage & Divorce Registry: The system must digitally register marriages and divorces in accordance with Sharia and government norms, securely storing the digitized documents and generating official certificates.
- NOCs & Clearance Certificates: If a devotee is marrying at a different mosque, your system must generate a localized "No Objection Certificate" (NOC) to submit to the hosting mosque. Similarly, it needs to issue "Clearance Certificates" for members who are relocating and transferring their registration to another parish.
- Death Registry: The system must record death details (including burial details and cause of death) and issue death certificates.

5. Meeting & Dispute (Panchayath) Management

- To ensure transparent governance, the system should include a digital logbook for committee activities.
- Immutable Meeting Minutes: The system must record the minutes of all Jamath (general) and Management committee meetings. Once the next meeting's minutes are stored, the system must "auto-protect" previous records, locking them from future edits to ensure data integrity.
- Panchayath Tracking: A specialized feature to track community dispute resolutions (Panchayath). It should log the application from the involved parties, track the progress of counselling meetings, and document the final resolution.
