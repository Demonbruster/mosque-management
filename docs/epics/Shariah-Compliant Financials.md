Since we decided to skip the payment gateway for the initial launch and have staff manually enter records, this epic focuses entirely on building a robust, secure ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) module for financial tracking.

- Manual Entry Dashboard: You need to build forms for staff to log incoming donations (cash or checks) and outgoing expenses. The system must force the admin to strictly tag the exact fund category from a dropdown (e.g., Zakat, Sadaqah, or General Building Fund) before saving, ensuring strict Shariah compliance in how funds are directed.
- Maker-Checker Workflow: Because humans are handling physical cash and manual data entry, the system needs a dual-authorization rule to prevent errors or fraud. When a staff member (the "Maker") logs a cash donation, it should remain in a "Pending" state until a senior official like the Treasurer (the "Checker") verifies the physical cash and approves the digital entry in the system.
- Automated Digital Receipts: The moment a transaction is approved, your Hono backend should automatically generate a PDF tax receipt and instantly send it to the donor's WhatsApp (via Twilio) or email. This saves administrative time and provides donors with immediate proof of their contribution.
- Public Financial Dashboard: A read-only, visual dashboard accessible to the congregation that displays monthly income versus expenses. This is critical for building donor trust and aligns with standards like ISAK 35, which emphasize that high financial transparency in mosques directly increases community confidence and future donations.
